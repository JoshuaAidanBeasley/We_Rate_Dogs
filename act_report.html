The above figure shows the average ratings for a 10,000 samples of 100 dogs for each category. On starting this project I thought floofers would be the top rated dog, as I am a follower of We_Rate_Dogs and he often rates floofers 13/10. However while the spread of puppo means is larger than that of floofers, it's evident by their positions that puppo's get the highest overall ratings. Floofers and doggos get a similar average rating (with doggos having a higher deviation, and finally puppers on average get the lowest rating.

With all mean ratings being well above 10/10 though, I'd say they really likes all the dogs.

Other¶

On running a multiple linear regression analysis with the dog categories as the x-axis and public activity as the y-axis, it was evident that categories pupper and puppo saw much more public activity than the other two categories, however, the model only accounted for 6.4% of variance. Due to a lack of retweet and favourite information available the model could only include about 10% of the population of tweets. For that reasons I decided not to continue with this line of analysis.
